Ukulele Beginner Tutorial

Learn the basics: parts, tuning, chords, and your first song.

1. Know the parts of your ukulele

Before playing, it helps to know the main parts of your ukulele: the headstock, tuning pegs, neck, frets, fretboard, body, and sound hole.

Basic illustration of a soprano/concert ukulele.

Tip: Hold the ukulele close to your chest with your strumming hand above the sound hole and your fretting hand on the neck.

2. Learn the standard tuning (G C E A)

Most beginner ukuleles use standard “re-entrant” tuning: from top string (closest to your face) to bottom string: G - C - E - A.

G 4th string
C 3rd string
E 2nd string
A 1st string
Tip: Tune slowly and always tune up to the pitch.

3. Your first chords: C, F, and G

C major chord

Place your ring finger on the 3rd fret of the A string.

G: 0
C: 0
E: 0
A: 3

F major chord

Index on E1, middle on G2.

G: 2
C: 0
E: 1
A: 0

G major chord

Index on C2, middle on A2, ring on E3.

G: 0
C: 2
E: 3
A: 2

4. Basic strumming pattern

Start with a simple down-strum pattern.

Try: Down, Down-Up, Up-Down-Up.

5. Your first simple song pattern

C → F → G → C (4 beats each)
