Ukulele Beginner Tutorial

Learn the basics: parts, tuning, chords, and your first song.

1. Know the parts of your ukulele

Before playing, it helps to know the main parts of your ukulele: the headstock, tuning pegs, neck, frets, fretboard, body, and sound hole.

Basic illustration of a soprano/concert ukulele.

Tip: Hold the ukulele close to your chest with your strumming hand above the sound hole and your fretting hand on the neck.

2. Learn the standard tuning (G C E A)

Most beginner ukuleles use standard “re-entrant” tuning: from top string (closest to your face) to bottom string: G - C - E - A.

G 4th string
C 3rd string
E 2nd string
A 1st string

Use a tuner app and match each open string to its note:

  • 4th string (top): G
  • 3rd string: C
  • 2nd string: E
  • 1st string (bottom): A
Tip: Pluck gently near the sound hole when tuning and always tune up to the pitch (if you go too high, tune down below the correct note and then slowly tune up).

3. Your first chords: C, F, and G

C major chord

Place your ring finger on the 3rd fret of the A string (1st string). The other strings are open.

C chord (top to bottom strings):
G: 0
C: 0
E: 0
A: 3

F major chord

Place your index finger on the 1st fret of the E string (2nd string), and your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the G string (4th string). The other strings are open.

F chord (top to bottom strings):
G: 2
C: 0
E: 1
A: 0

G major chord

Place your index finger on the 2nd fret of the C string (3rd string), your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the A string (1st string), and your ring finger on the 3rd fret of the E string (2nd string).

G chord (top to bottom strings):
G: 0
C: 2
E: 3
A: 2
Practice: Slowly change between C → F → G → C. At first, do a down-strum, then switch chords, down-strum again, and so on.

4. Basic strumming pattern

Start with a simple “Down Down Down Down” pattern in a steady rhythm, counting “1 - 2 - 3 - 4”.

  • Step 1: Rest your strumming hand lightly above the sound hole.
  • Step 2: Use your index finger nail or thumb to brush down across all strings.
  • Step 3: Keep your wrist relaxed and move from the wrist, not the elbow.
  • Step 4: Try: C chord for 4 beats, F for 4, G for 4, then back to C for 4.
Try this pattern: Down, Down-Up, Up-Down-Up (often written as D – DU – UDU). Play it slowly and evenly.

5. Your first simple song pattern

Here is a simple 4-bar chord progression you can loop and sing anything over:

  • Bar 1: C (4 beats)
  • Bar 2: F (4 beats)
  • Bar 3: G (4 beats)
  • Bar 4: C (4 beats)

Use the “Down, Down-Up, Up-Down-Up” pattern on each chord. Start slow, then gradually increase your speed as you get more comfortable.
