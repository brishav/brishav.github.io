Guitar Beginner Tutorial

A structured, beginner-friendly guide to help you start playing confidently.

1. Understanding the Parts of a Guitar

Knowing the parts of your guitar helps you follow lessons, tune correctly, and understand instructions. Here are the essential components:

  • Headstock: Holds the tuning pegs.
  • Tuning Pegs: Used to tighten or loosen strings.
  • Nut: Guides the strings from the headstock to the neck.
  • Neck: Long wooden part where you place your fingers.
  • Frets: Metal strips that divide the neck into notes.
  • Fretboard: The surface where your fingers press the strings.
  • Body: The large hollow part that amplifies sound.
  • Sound Hole: Where sound resonates (acoustic guitars).
  • Bridge: Anchors the strings to the body.
Beginner Tip: Sit upright, rest the guitar on your right leg (if right‑handed), and keep the neck slightly angled upward for comfort.

2. Standard Guitar Tuning (E A D G B E)

A guitar has six strings. From thickest (top) to thinnest (bottom), the notes are:

  • 6th string: E (Low E)
  • 5th string: A
  • 4th string: D
  • 3rd string: G
  • 2nd string: B
  • 1st string: E (High E)
Use a tuner app. Pluck each string gently and adjust the tuning peg until the tuner shows the correct note.
Common mistake: Turning the peg too fast. Always tune slowly to avoid breaking strings.

4. Basic Strumming Technique

Strumming is about rhythm, not force. Keep your wrist loose and let your hand glide across the strings.

Beginner Pattern

Down – Down-Up – Up-Down-Up

Count it as: 1 – 2& – &4&. Start slow and keep your hand moving even when not hitting the strings.

Common mistake: Strumming from the elbow. Use your wrist for smoother motion.

5. Your First Song Progression

Use the chords you learned to play this simple 4‑chord progression:

  • C (4 beats)
  • G (4 beats)
  • Em (4 beats)
  • D (4 beats)

Play each chord with the strumming pattern above. Repeat the loop and try singing over it once you're comfortable.

Daily Practice: 10 min chords • 10 min strumming • 10 min switching between chords.
